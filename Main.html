I am delighted to present to you the first monthly newsletter from the SEC. In an effort to improve our relationships with past members, as well as keeping all within the college of engineering informed about the council, we have established this brand new form of communication.

Around the Council

Intoducing the '19/'20 Executive Board
The SEC has officially completed it's election of the new executive board for the 2019/2020 academic year. The election process took place over two general council meetings this month beginning with the Presidential Election. The council elected Abhi Dillion (Class of 2020) as their leader for the upcoming year.
 
The second meeting saw the election of the Vice-Presidents for each divsion of the council. Sabrina Padoe (Class of 2020) will take on the role of Vice-President of Development. Kiersten Potter (Class of 2020) will be the incoming Vice-President of External. Finally, Justis Morales (Class of 2021) will be the Vice-President of Internal.  
 
The new Executive Board went straight to work and rounded off their team with the selection of their Executive Secretary, Nadia Whitehouse (Class of 2021). 
 
We wish the new team the best of luck and cannot wait to see the amazing things they achieve in the upcoming year. 
 

Membership Hub
4,991 lines of code, 474 hours of work and one committed member. MembershipHub has launch and replaces the old and very much outdated SEC members website. The website boasts a whole series of new features, such as meeting check-in and points tracking, and is set to make life for the entire council much easier.
 
The project was started in Fall 2018 by Dominick Fabian (Class of 2019), originally as part of a software engineering project. He continued to develop the website long after the project was completed and the entire coucil couldn't be more grateful. MembershipHub is ther first of a series of "hub" set to help manage the SEC. 
 

Development

First DI Saturday in Zach

The Department Informationals provides freshmen engineers the opportunity to hear from each department, as they make their decision on which major path they will undertake. Previously the informationals took place in different locations throughout the campus, however this year it was located all within the brand-new Zachary Engineering Education Complex.
 
Students were able to attending each informational they desired, without losing time trying to navigate between buildings. The new format was praised by departments alike who saw increases in the attendance to each of their sessions. The SEC coordinated the day and we were glad to see so many freshmen out, keen take in as much information as possible as they make their big decision going into sophomore year.  
 
A thank you to all those who helped and gave up time on their Saturday and we wish all the engineering freshmen the best of luck in choosing their majors.

E-Week 2019

E-Week went big this year with a series of events celebrating all things engineering throughout the week. It kicked off with a breakfast of coffee and hundreds of donuts in the Zachary E-Quad, providing engineers with an initial boost to get them through the week.
 
This was followed up by a mid-week stress reducer, which included food, games, yoga and even therapy dogs! Also, for the first time ever throughout the week the SEC was selling official apparel made in collaboration with the College of Engineering.  
 
The week culminated with the big Friday Finale, which brought many of the engineering organizations together to celebrate E-Week. Held in the Zachry ConocoPhillips Atrium and Chevron Rooms, students enjoyed a free lunch of variety of foods including pizza roles and Canes. The fun was endless with each student organization setting up interactive booths, where students were challenged to name all the parts of a rocket and solve puzzles to earn raffle tickets. The raffle drew in big crowds as students swung by to see if they were lucky enough to take away one of the amazing prizes, which included Amazon Fire Tablets and a Kuerig.  
 
A big thank you to everyone who helped out with E-Week this year, it certainly would not have been such a great week without help from our volunteers. E-Week was a huge success and were already counting down the days to the next one.  
 

Internal

Inaugral ESO Open House

Continuing the theme of a series of firsts for the SEC, the first Engineering Student Organizations Open House was held earlier in the month. This brand-new event, set to be a bi-annual tradition, was the brainchild of Ryan Zimmerman and Miles Hubscher. The Open House provides students in the College of Engineering a more intimate engagement with engineering societies, with each one being organized by major.
 
The event was a huge success, with many students attending and joining a variety of different organizations. Once again this was held again inside the Zachary Engineering Education Complex, providing a central location for engineers of all disciplines. The next ESO Open House is scheduled for early in the upcoming fall semester.  
 

Engineering Student Survey Results

Once again the Engineering Student Survey was conducted by the SEC and presented to the College of Engineering. For the first time ever the SEC worked in collaboration with TAMU's Public Policy Research Insitute. This allowed us to have a qualified third-party vet the survey and help make it as unbiased and effective as possible.
 
The survey was conducted over a five and a half week period at the end of the fall semester. We received a total of 6696 responses, which is approximately 34% of the engineering student body.

History Corner
80 Years and Counting
The SEC is one of the oldest student organizations on campus, orginally established in 1939. Since then it has been the causes of many changes within the College of Engineering, always striving to be a voice for the student body.
 
Despite some of the major achievements of the Council big or small, such as sponsorship of the entire 4th floor of the Zachary Engineering Education Complex, many of these have been left undocumented. That is why the counci has established the new role of a Council Archivist, whose purpose will be to not only go back through the years and record the history of thecouncil, but also ensure the present is well documented. This will provide future members a better understanding of past decisions and events, which will hopefully assist them in their running of the council. 
 
This position is just getting started, so be on the lookout in for this section of the newsletter, where we will have profiles on previous prominent members, as well as a where are they now. Are you a previous member of the SEC and have some stories to share? Please do not hesistate to contact the Archivst at: archivist@sec.tamu.edu, we would love to hear from you!
